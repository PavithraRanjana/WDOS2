Visit an ancient kingdom at Sigiriya


Sigiriya is a UNESCO world heritage and often call the 8th wonder of the world. It was the home to King Kashyapa during his reign. Sigiriya is a massive city built on top of a huge granite rock. The surrounding area is covered with ponds and parks. Sigiriya is a marvel of ancient urban planing and engineering because of how complex the structure really is.

Our Hotel will arange all the transport you need to go there. You will also be provided with a guide make sure you explore everything Sigiriya has to offer.


when you get to the top of the rock fortress you will get amazing scenic views of the thick forest sourounding the area.


Enjoy a Safari adventure in Minneriya National Park


The main attraction of the Minneriya National Park is the large elephant gathering during the dry season. You will get to experience the elephants in their natural habitat instead of a sanctuary environment. Elephants migrate from Wasgamuwa national park to Minneriya in search for food and water.

Aprat from seeing asian elephants you will also see large number of native birds, mamels. Leopards are a common sight in the Minneriya national park.

Our experience safari drivers and guides will provide you with a safe and informative safari through Minneriya National Park. Hotel will also provide all the food and beverages during your safari.

Have an Adventure of Whale Watching


Mirissa sea is the best sea for whale watching not only within Sri Lanka but also in the world. At Mirissa you can see Blue Whales, Sperm Whales and Killer Whales too. Mirissa is one of the few places you can see all these whales at a single place. And if you are lucky you might see Humpback Whales as well.

In addition to whales you are most likely to see other marine life. Such as Groups of dolphins and fly fish.

Our hotel operates it's own boats, so you can go whale watching comfortably and without any hassle. Our boats can carry 8 people at a time. And operated by experienced captain who know how to find the Whales.
